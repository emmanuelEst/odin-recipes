Kung Pao Chicken

Description

A simple pan fried chicken recipe with a bit of a kick. In three simple steps, you can have a delicious component to your meal that pairs well with any of your favorite rice or vegetable sides.

This dish is also forgiving allowing you to stray a bit from the recipe and still have great results.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons white wine
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil, divided
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons of water
  • 1 ounce hot chili paste
  • 1 teaspoon chopped garlic
  • 1 (8 ounce) can water chestnut
  • 1 teaspoon distilled vinegar
  • 2 teaspoon brown sugar
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 4 ounces chopped peanuts

Steps

  1. Make marinade: Combine 1 tablespoon wine, soy sauce, oil, and cornstarch/water mix. Add chicken in a bowl the pour marinade on chicken. Toss to coat. Cover marinaded chicken and place in refrigerator for 30 minutes
  2. Make sauce: Combine 1 tablespoon of wine, soy sauce, oil and cornstarch/water mix in a bowl. Add 1 tablespoon chilli paste, vinegar, and sugar. Mix together and add green onion, garlic, water chestnut and peanuts. In a medium skillet, heat sauce until it becomes aromatic.
  3. Remove chicken from marinade and saute in a skillet until meat is white and juices run clear. Add chicken to the sauce and simmer together until sauce thickens.
